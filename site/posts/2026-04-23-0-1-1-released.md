0.1.1 is live on [cerberix.org](/) and on the
[SourceForge mirror](https://sourceforge.net/projects/cerberix-linux/files/0.1.1/).
SHA256 and detached signature are up next to the ISO as always.

Yesterday's post said the next build would ship `haveged` to stop
the install hang on low-entropy VMs, and it does. Before cutting the
release I actually sat down and installed it, end-to-end, in a fresh
headless QEMU — something I should have done before 0.1.0 and didn't.
Lesson learned there.

Here's where it gets a bit awkward.

## The fix is there, but it's not really doing anything on modern kernels

`haveged` has a systemd condition: `ConditionVersion=<5.6`. On any
kernel from 5.6 onwards, systemd just skips it — the in-kernel CRNG
got a rewrite around that version and seeds itself fast enough that
`haveged` is redundant. Cerberix ships kernel 6.19. So in my test VM,
`haveged` was installed, enabled, symlinked into
`sysinit.target.wants`, started defensively inside the installer — and
systemd quietly skipped all of it every time. The service literally
never ran.

## I never reproduced the original hang

`pacman-key --init` into a clean gnupg dir took **three seconds** on
the 0.1.1 live ISO. Into an already-initialized dir, a fraction of a
second. Neither is within an order of magnitude of the two-hour hang
the original reporter described.

Which means one of two things:

1. The person who reported it was on an older kernel, or a
   virtualization stack that somehow starves the CRNG badly even on
   6.x. In which case `haveged` being present (even conditionally
   dormant) is still a belt-and-braces improvement.
2. The hang was something else entirely — `pacman-key`'s keyserver
   fetch timing out against a slow WKD lookup, for instance, looks
   identical from the outside. Same "sits forever at the pacman-key
   step" symptom, totally different root cause. A `haveged` fix would
   do nothing for that.

I genuinely don't know which it is. I'd guess (2) is more likely on
a modern kernel, but I can't prove it without the original reporter's
environment in front of me.

## So what does 0.1.1 actually give you

- A clean install, verified end-to-end in QEMU
- `haveged` in the base set, ready to kick in on older kernels or odd
  virtualization setups where it actually matters
- The version bumped in the right places (os-release, site, release
  notes, grub distributor string)
- Updated SHA256 and GPG signature

If you were on 0.1.0 and things worked fine — 0.1.1 is not urgent.
Grab it at your leisure. If you hit the install hang on 0.1.0, 0.1.1
might still stall in the same spot, and if it does I want to hear
about it at [bugs@cerberix.org](mailto:bugs@cerberix.org) because
then we're looking at the second possibility above and I'd like to
chase it properly.

Thanks to everyone who wrote in on the 0.1.0 release — keep it coming,
including the "this didn't work for me" ones. Those are the ones that
move the needle.
